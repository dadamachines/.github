# dadamachines

Founded by [Johannes Elias Lohbihler](https://lohbihler.com) — creative technologist in Berlin, seventh generation carpenter from Bavaria, raised in the Weilheim music scene alongside Portmanteau, Lali Puna, and The Notwist. DJ turned creator, bridging analog craft and electronic music.

dadamachines is a studio for multisensory experiences & open-source music technology, supported by a network of collaborators and friends building experimental sound tools for real life.

Our current project is the [**TBD-16**](https://dadamachines.github.io/ctag-tbd/) — a standalone desktop instrument with 50+ DSP plugins, standard MIDI, browser UI, and Ableton Link. Built on [CTAG TBD](https://github.com/ctag-fh-kiel/ctag-tbd) by Robert Manzke. Fully open source (GPL/LGPL).

🌐 [dadamachines.com](https://dadamachines.com) · 📖 [Docs](https://dadamachines.github.io/ctag-tbd/) · 💬 [Contact](https://dadamachines.com/contact/)

## Firmware & Tools

- [**ctag-tbd**](https://github.com/dadamachines/ctag-tbd) — ESP32-P4 audio DSP engine, UI/UX, and docs
- [**dada-tbd-firmware**](https://github.com/dadamachines/dada-tbd-firmware) — Pre-built firmware binaries
- [**dada-tbd-tui**](https://github.com/dadamachines/dada-tbd-tui) — Terminal-based update wizard

## Apps & Templates

- [**dada-tbd-app-template**](https://github.com/dadamachines/dada-tbd-app-template) — Starting point for RP2350 apps (Arduino & PlatformIO)
- [**dada-tbd-doom**](https://github.com/dadamachines/dada-tbd-doom) — DOOM 1 running on the TBD-16
